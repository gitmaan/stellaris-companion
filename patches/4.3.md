# Stellaris 4.3 "Cetus" Game Mechanics

<!-- LLM-transformed from open beta and live patch notes (2025-12-11 through 2026-04-29). Strategic implications included. -->

### Fleet & Naval Capacity
* **Ship Naval Capacity:** All ships use approximately 5x more Naval Capacity. Corvettes: 5, Frigates: 8, Destroyers: 10, Cruisers: 20, Battleships: 40, Titans: 80, Juggernauts/Colossi: 100. Base empire Naval Capacity is 50. (Fleets contain far fewer ships; each ship choice matters significantly more.)
* **Anchorages:** Provide +5 Naval Capacity (+3 with Naval Logistics Office) but incur Trade upkeep equal to their Naval Capacity contribution. (Naval capacity has ongoing economic cost.)
* **Naval Capacity Settings:** Galaxy settings can scale Naval Capacity and Command Limit from x0.5 to x5.0, multiplicative with other modifiers. Hard caps remain 9999 Naval Capacity and 1000 Command Limit. (Custom galaxy settings can drastically alter fleet-size expectations.)
* **Naval Capacity Penalties:** Exceeding Naval Capacity applies the intended ship-upkeep penalty. (Over-cap fleets create real economic pressure.)
* **Frigates:** Frigates move at Destroyer speed with additional Utility and Aux slots and Destroyer-sized reactors. (Significantly more capable and versatile.)

### Combat
* **Summon Dimensional Fleet:** This astral action summons Escorts equal to the number of explored Astral Rifts, and Cruisers equal to half that number rounded down. (With 10 rifts: 10 Escorts + 5 Cruisers. Rewards sustained rift exploration.)
* **MIA Fleets:** Fleets can remain Missing in Action for at most 10 years except Phased fleets, Eaten fleets, and fleets exploring Astral Rifts. (Very long MIA timers eventually resolve.)
* **Perdition Beams:** Titan Perdition Beams and Ancient Ruination Glare deal collateral damage to ships near the target. Ion Cannons deal collateral damage of 100 over shorter range. (Titans threaten groups of ships, not just single targets.)
* **Armor & Shield Hardening:** Higher-tier armor and shields grant (tier - 1)% Armor or Shield Hardening. (Late-game defenses scale better against raw damage.)
* **Disruptors:** Start at 50% shield and armor bypass at tier 1, increasing to 80% at Psionic tier, plus +25% damage against shields. Disruptor hits also reduce regeneration for 10 days: T1 reduces Shield by 20% and Armor/Hull by 10%; T2 reduces Shield by 35% and Armor/Hull by 20%; T3 reduces Shield by 50% and Armor/Hull by 30%; Psionic reduces Shield by 35% and Fire Rate by 20%. (Strong generalist weapons that suppress regeneration.)
* **Siege Computers:** Torpedo Combat Computers are renamed Siege Computers and add +50% to +100% damage vs. Starbases plus increased Bombardment Damage. (Dedicated siege fleets are far more effective.)
* **Eternal Vigilance:** No longer creates free defense platforms. Instead grants +20% home territory ship fire rate. (Defensive perk favors mobile fleets over static defenses.)
* **Ground Combat:** Army counts are generally reduced. Many weapon and defensive technologies also improve army health or damage. Orbital bombardment damage is slightly increased.
* **Crisis Purges:** Purges from threats such as Prethoryn and Sentinels pause while ground battles are active. (Landing armies can buy time for threatened pops.)
* **Gravity Storms:** Gravity Storm damage penalties scale down for larger ships. (Large hulls weather storms better than small hulls.)

### Economy & Jobs
* **No Unemployment:** All unemployed pops demote to Civilian (or Maintenance Drone) at end of month. Resettlement costs are uniform across strata. (No unemployment management needed; no demotion timers.)
* **Technology Costs:** Halved across the board, but research output from jobs is also reduced. Space-based research deposits are relatively more valuable. (Exploration and expansion for research deposits matters more.)
* **Stability:** Most stability bonuses reduced to one-half to one-third of previous values. Reaching 100% stability is significantly harder. (Even small stability effects like +1 or +2 are meaningful investments.)
* **Job Efficiency:** Most job efficiency modifiers are reduced. Colony Designations now universally grant Job Efficiency to relevant jobs. Planetary Ascension increases the Designation effect by +10% per tier. (Designation choice is a key strategic decision.)
* **Soldiers:** Now Specialist jobs (Complex Drone for gestalts) with 1 Alloy upkeep. Soldiers, Battle Thralls, and Warrior Drones provide 4 Naval Capacity for the same 1 Alloy upkeep. Strong, Very Strong, and Battle Thrall traits affect Soldier efficiency. (Standing armies and naval-cap jobs have real economic cost.)
* **Empire Size:** Base Empire Size from pops is halved; from colonies is increased to 20. Most Empire Size reduction modifiers are reduced. (Wide play is slightly more penalized per colony but less per pop.)
* **Special Empire Size Reductions:** Wilderness has a lower colony Empire Size penalty than the 4.3 baseline. Sovereign Guardianship variants, including Guardian Matrix, also reduce the colony Empire Size penalty. (Some wide-averse builds are less colony-punished than the generic rule suggests.)
* **Automation:** Automation buildings are job-specific with scaling Energy upkeep: 4 per 100 Worker jobs, 8 per 100 Specialist jobs, 12 per 100 Elite jobs. Only Fallen Empire buildings automate Specialist jobs. (Automation is a significant energy investment; Fallen Empire buildings are uniquely powerful.)
* **Planetary Automation:** Automation can build districts and non-job buildings that match the colony build list, checks at 50 civilians, and is best on focused resource colonies. The Prevent Deficit Construction setting can stop automation from building. (Automation needs focused designations and deficit rules reviewed.)
* **Unity & Factions:** Many Unity sources reduced; base production increased to 10. Factions have 50% reduced output during the first 10 years. (Unity income grows more slowly in the early game.)
* **Trade:** Branch Offices and private colony ships cost Trade instead of Energy. Subsidy Edicts grant +10-20% Job Efficiency with Trade upkeep. (Trade is more central to the economy.)
* **Ecumenopolis:** The Arcology Project and Ecumenopolis districts cost Alloys instead of Minerals. (Requires alloy stockpiling for transition.)
* **Living Standards:** Happiness penalties on lower living standards now apply to Civilians. (Living standard choice has broader impact on empire happiness.)
* **Buildings:** Slave Processing Facility, Orbital Slave Processing Hub, Administrative Complex variants, Data-Driven Theorem Facility, Distributed Design Center, and Metallurgical Research Lab provide 10% efficiency bonuses. Ministry of Production and Production Center provide 0.2 Alloy and Consumer Goods bonuses. Advanced Bio-Reactors produce 0.1 Exotic Gases. (Several economy buildings are stronger anchors for specialization.)
* **Living Metal Deficits:** A Living Metal shortage reduces Job Output by 30%. (Living Metal deficits are severe even when the tooltip is the only visible warning.)
* **Habitats:** High-tier habitat capitals can gain +1 habitat district per major body. (Mature habitat systems scale better around major bodies.)

### Ethics
* **Authoritarian:** Worker Production bonus is now Worker Job Efficiency. **Egalitarian:** Specialist Production bonus is now Specialist Job Efficiency. **Pacifist:** Empire Size from Pops reduced to -10% (Pacifist) / -20% (Fanatic Pacifist).

### Species Traits
* **Broad Rebalance:** Many traits reduced from 30-50% bonuses to 15-25%. Examples: Erudite 20% researcher efficiency, Fertile 20% pop growth, Dark Matter Engines 20% job efficiency. Rare resource upkeep costs on advanced traits are generally increased. (No single trait dominates; trait selection is more about synergy than raw power.)
* **Cybernetic Traits:** No longer have flat energy upkeep. The Cybernetic trait swaps half of base food upkeep for energy.

### Origins & Civics
* **Necrophage:** Chambers of Elevation block necrophage species growth. Necrophyte Apprentices boost non-necrophage growth on the same planet. Mass conversion occurs via once-per-decade rituals. (Grow secondary species, then batch-convert.)
* **Driven Assimilators:** Start with 2,000 cyborg pops and 1,000 fewer machine pops. Augmentation Centers provide 400 jobs with Amenities and Organic Pop Growth for cyborgs. (More cyborg-focused from game start.)
* **Cybernetic Ascension:** Metabolic Reprocessing enables cross-assembly: robot assembly from Augmentors, organic assembly from Roboticists. New cyborg traits for pop growth and bureaucrat output. (Flexible workforce strategy across organic and synthetic pops.)
* **Cybernetic Creed:** Augmentation sub-civics provide +10% Job Efficiency to specific job types plus Bureaucrats. Creed traits are no longer locked to Spiritualist ethics. (More flexible empire design options.)
* **Machine Cybernetic Leaders:** Cybernetic governors in Machine empires boost Pop Assembly rate rather than workforce efficiency. Driven Assimilator robotic leaders retain the Cyborg trait, and fast Cybernetics completion still reaches the Advanced Assimilators government choice. (Machine-cyborg planning should value assembly support.)
* **Virtuality & Modularity:** Virtuality bonus jobs scale per district. Modularity's soldier stability bonus provides +0.25 Stability per 100 Soldiers. (Virtual and modular empires depend on district and soldier scale rather than flat planet assumptions.)
* **Machine World Trade:** Individual Machine empires can assign Machine Worlds to Trade Colony designation, and colony automation handles trade districts and specializations on those worlds. (Machine trade colonies are valid specialization targets.)
* **Eater of Worlds:** Doom power summons a fleet instead of starting a war. Fleet scales with Doom Intensity: escorts at 3-6, plus cruisers at 6-12, plus battleships at 12+. Intensity caps at 50. Summoning a new fleet dismisses the current one. (Fleet-summoning covenant, not war-trigger.)
* **Refugee Attraction:** Civics like Free Haven increase refugee flow; allowing slavery reduces it. Refugees prefer empires ruled by their own species. (Xenophile/egalitarian empires gain more population through migration.)

### Crises & Late Game
* **Crisis Fleets:** Prethoryn, Contingency, Extradimensionals, Great Khan, and other crisis fleets reduced by approximately one-third. Awakened Fallen Empire and Cetana fleets reduced by roughly half. (End-game crises are less overwhelming.)
* **Created Fleets:** GDF reinforcement fleets, revolts, machine uprisings, civil wars, Habinte, and a Zevox ending use 4.3 ship-size math rather than accidentally creating fleets around five times larger than intended. Doom of the Eater fleets are larger to compensate. (Event fleet strength is less likely to spike from old ship-count assumptions.)
* **Cosmogenesis:** Fallen Empires directly oppose experiments from stage 5, culminating in Total War. (Military preparation against Fallen Empires is required for Cosmogenesis.)
* **Voidworms:** Voidworm fleet power better reflects their actual capability, especially when countered with Point Defense. Voidworms are weaker on Civilian and Cadet difficulty. (Point Defense is a stronger practical counter.)

### Specialist Buildings & DLC-Specific Corrections
* **Oppressive Autocracy Medical Centers:** Oppressive Autocracies can build Medical Centers, but Medical Workers and Genomic Researchers remain Specialist jobs and only Elite stratum jobs generate amenities.
* **Astral Siphons:** Astral Siphons produce +0.2, +0.35, and +0.50 Astral Threads, provide the same values as Physics bonuses, add +200, +400, and +600 Thread storage, and carry reduced additional upkeep of +1/+1.5/+2 Consumer Goods or +2/+3/+4 Energy/Minerals for gestalts.
* **Faculty of Archaeostudies:** On Relic Worlds, Biologists from Faculty of Archaeostudies produce +0.25 Minor Artifacts. Archaeo-Engineering adds +0.15 Biologist Minor Artifact production, raises Minor Artifact storage to 3000, and the base building storage cap is 2000.
* **Grand Archive Culling:** Grand Archive pop-culling events remove 20% of the affected pop group, not a flat 20 pops. (The impact scales with the affected population group.)
* **Open Shroud Rift:** The Open Shroud Rift planetary decision requires a valid planet type at use; queued decisions cannot bypass planet-type restrictions through terraforming. (Do not plan around bypassing Shroud Rift restrictions.)
* **Tankbound Void Dwellers:** Tankbound empires with Void Dwellers use reduced starting pops and converted habitat districts appropriate to the origin. (Their opening economy should not be judged from standard Void Dweller assumptions alone.)
* **Offworld Prospectors:** Offworld Prospectors jobs from Galvanic Synthesis do not produce negative Alloys while Centrifugal Siphoning is active. (This civic/edict pair is not self-sabotaging.)
