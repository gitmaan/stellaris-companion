# Stellaris 4.3 "Cetus" Current Mechanics Snapshot

<!-- Compiled cumulative snapshot through 4.3. Uses only current-state facts for prompt injection. -->

### Population & Workforce Reference

| Milestone | Current Scale |
|-----------|---------------|
| Empire start | 2,000 pops |
| New colony | 100-500 pops |
| Colony established | 100 pops |
| Underdeveloped colony | under 1,000 pops |
| Typical mid-game empire | 80,000-120,000 pops |
| Typical late-game empire | 200,000+ pops |

These population numbers are normal. Large pop counts are expected in 4.x and are not alarming by themselves.

| Workforce Rule | Current Value |
|----------------|---------------|
| Workforce per pop | 1 |
| Workforce per job | about 100 |
| Job efficiency | scales effective job capacity, not direct output |

About 100 pops are needed to fill one job. Advice about "1 pop = 1 job" is incorrect in 4.x.

### Core Economy & Population Rules

* **Strata & Demotion:** The main strata are Elites, Specialist, Worker, and Civilian. Gestalts use Maintenance Drones instead of Civilians. All unemployed pops demote to Civilian or Maintenance Drone at the end of the month.
* **Migration & Refugees:** Civilians and unemployed pops auto-migrate. Refugees prefer empires ruled by their own species when possible. Refugee Attraction matters: civics such as Free Haven attract more refugees, while allowing slavery makes an empire less attractive as a refuge.
* **Living Standards & Resettlement:** Happiness penalties on lower living standards apply to Civilians. Resettlement costs are uniform across strata. Social Welfare is less generous than older expectations, especially for happiness and civilian Unity.
* **Trade Currency:** Trade is the Galactic Market currency. Energy is a normal resource that is bought and sold on the market.
* **Trade Deficits:** Planetary deficits create Trade upkeep. Trade deficits reduce Job Efficiency and increase Empire Size pressure, so persistent Trade shortages are strategically dangerous.
* **Empire Size:** Pops generate less Empire Size than before, but colonies generate 20 Empire Size each. Wide expansion is constrained more by colony count than by raw population.
* **Jobs & Scaling:** Effects that used to grant jobs per pop are now mostly static job sources. Economic planning depends less on population-scaled job creation and more on fixed buildings, districts, and specializations.
* **Unity, Research, and Stability:** Base Unity is 10. Many Unity and job-output modifiers are smaller, most stability bonuses are weaker, and 100 Stability is harder to sustain. Research from many jobs is lower, while technology costs are much lower, making space-based deposits relatively more valuable.
* **Edicts & Efficiency:** Subsidy edicts use Efficiency bonuses with Trade upkeep rather than being pure output boosts.

### Colonies, Designations & Automation

* **Colony Establishment:** Colony ships deposit 100 pops. A colony counts as established at 100 pops, and the early development period still lasts about 2 years and 10 months with no production or growth.
* **Designations & Ascension:** Colony Designations add Job Efficiency to their relevant jobs. Planetary Ascension increases the Designation effect by 10% per tier.
* **District Specializations:** City districts support 2 specialization slots; Generator, Mining, and Farming districts support 1 each. A specialization slot provides 100 jobs and opens 3 building slots.
* **Planet Automation Scope:** Job automation is planet-wide by job type. Technician automation applies to all Technician jobs on the planet, not only jobs from Generator Districts.
* **Automation Buildings:** Automation buildings are job-specific and no longer scale upkeep from district count. Automated Worker or Simple Drone jobs add 4 Energy upkeep per 100 jobs, Specialist or Complex Drone jobs add 8, and Elite jobs add 12.
* **Automation Unlock Timing:** Assembly Patterns is a Tier 2 technology and Construction Templates is a Tier 4 technology.
* **Strategic Resource Specialization:** Strategic resource production is concentrated more heavily in strategic-resource district specializations, and raw deposits matter less without the matching specialization.
* **Planetary Deposits:** Special planetary deposits are more common than before, while space mineral and energy deposits are somewhat better than older expectations.

### Naval Capacity & Fleet Scale

| Ship Class | Naval Capacity |
|------------|----------------|
| Corvette | 5 |
| Frigate | 8 |
| Destroyer | 10 |
| Cruiser | 20 |
| Battleship | 40 |
| Titan / Bio-Titan | 80 |
| Juggernaut / Colossus | 100 |

Most normal ships use roughly 5x the Naval Capacity of older pre-4.3 expectations. Base empire Naval Capacity is 50, so fleets are smaller in ship count and every hull matters more.

* **Anchorages:** Each Anchorage provides 5 Naval Capacity. Naval Logistics Office adds 3 more. Anchorages also create Trade upkeep equal to the Naval Capacity they provide.
* **Command Limit:** Many sources of Command Limit and Naval Capacity are stronger. Effects that used to give flat Command Limit often use multipliers instead.
* **Federation & GDF Scaling:** Federation fleets, GDF fleets, and related bonuses are tuned around the larger per-ship Naval Capacity values.
* **Frigates:** Frigates move at Destroyer speed, are sturdier, and have extra utility and auxiliary flexibility compared with older expectations.
* **Special Exceptions:** Bubbles does not consume Naval Capacity. Some special crisis or marauding fleets use reduced capacity relative to their hull size.

### Combat & Military Rules

* **Soldiers:** Soldiers are Specialist jobs for normal empires and Complex Drone jobs for gestalts. Soldier jobs have Alloy upkeep.
* **Armor, Shields, and Hardening:** Higher-tier armor and shields also grant a small amount of Armor or Shield Hardening. Late-game defenses are less about raw HP alone.
* **Crystal Plating:** Crystal hull plating adds flat damage reduction in addition to hull, so plating can blunt repeated small hits more effectively than older expectations.
* **Titan & Starbase Area Damage:** Perdition Beams deal collateral damage around the target. Ion Cannons also cause collateral damage, but over a shorter range. Heavy weapons punish clustered fleets.
* **Siege Computers:** Siege Combat Computers are the current torpedo-line computer. They add strong anti-starbase and bombardment bonuses.
* **Disruptors:** Disruptors begin with major shield and armor bypass and keep stronger bypass at higher tiers, while also dealing bonus damage to shields. They are reliable anti-defense weapons across the game.
* **Space Warfare Balance:** Larger ship sections, components, and advanced hulls are more expensive and harder to maintain. Crisis fleets are somewhat smaller on paper, but player fleets are also smaller, so endgame wars remain dangerous.
* **Ground Combat:** Army counts are generally lower, many technologies improve army combat stats, and orbital bombardment is somewhat stronger than older expectations.
* **Defense Perks:** Eternal Vigilance does not create free defense platforms. It improves combat in home territory instead.

### Trade, Corporations & Common Economic Questions

* **Branch Offices:** Branch Offices use Trade rather than Energy as their establishment resource. They remain limited and economically important strategic investments.
* **Slave Market:** Slaves on the market cost Trade rather than Energy.
* **Private Colony Ships:** Private colony ships use Trade rather than Energy.
* **Ecumenopolis Investment:** The Arcology Project and Ecumenopolis districts consume Alloys rather than Minerals, so conversion into an Ecumenopolis is a major alloy commitment.

### Ethics, Authorities & Tradition Names

* **Ethics Output Rules:** Authoritarian boosts Worker Job Efficiency, Egalitarian boosts Specialist Job Efficiency, and Pacifist reduces Empire Size from Pops less aggressively than older stacked-reduction expectations.
* **Corporate Authority:** Corporate governments gain an Envoy rather than an extra External Leader Pool slot.
* **Tradition Names:** The relevant tradition trees are Commerce and Fortification.
* **Specialist Training:** The Specialist Training tradition scales Specialist Job Efficiency with Planetary Governor level.

### Crisis & Late-Game Pressure

* **Multiple Endgame Crises:** If galaxy settings allow multiple endgame crises, another crisis can appear 25 years after a previous one spawns.
* **Crisis Relative Challenge:** Prethoryn, Contingency, Extradimensionals, Great Khan, Tempest-related threats, and several other late-game fleets are numerically lighter than before, but player fleet size also falls sharply under the 4.3 naval-cap system.
* **Cetana & Other Crisis Timing:** Cetana is less likely to appear while another crisis is active, and crisis scaling is tuned around the all-crises setting.
* **Cosmogenesis Pressure:** Cosmogenesis experiments face direct Fallen Empire opposition in the late stages, potentially escalating into Total War.
* **Combat Computer Name:** The torpedo-line combat computer is Siege Combat Computer.

### Frequently Asked Build-Specific Corrections

* **Cybernetic Economies:** Cybernetic traits do not carry flat energy upkeep. Cybernetic upkeep shifts part of normal biological upkeep into Energy, and cybernetic economies care about both food-equivalent upkeep and Energy.
* **Cybernetic Assembly:** Metabolic Reprocessing supports cross-assembly between Augmentors and Roboticists, so cybernetic empires can scale both organic and robotic population growth systems together.
* **Cybernetic Creed:** Cybernetic Creed is compatible with more ethics setups than older assumptions, including Egalitarian cases, and is not locked to Spiritualist-only planning.
* **Driven Assimilators:** Driven Assimilators begin with a heavier cyborg population mix than older expectations and lean harder into cyborg growth and augmentation infrastructure from the opening years.
* **Necrophage Growth:** Necrophage growth planning revolves around maintaining secondary-species growth and converting them through elevation cycles, not choking off all secondary-species population growth.
* **Dimensional Fleet Astral Action:** The current Dimensional Fleet action summons Escorts equal to explored Astral Rifts and Cruisers equal to half that number, rounded down.
* **Psionic Zro Deficits:** A Zro deficit severely weakens psionic economies and can trigger Shroud Incursions, so running negative Zro is strategically dangerous.
* **Eater of Worlds Covenant:** Doom manifests a fleet scaled by Doom Intensity instead of immediately starting a war.
* **Knights & Soldier Logic:** Knight jobs now behave as Soldier-type jobs for economic modifiers rather than as Researchers or Bureaucrats.
* **Oppressive Autocracy:** Enforcers and Telepaths belong to the Elite stratum rather than using a Specialist exception.
